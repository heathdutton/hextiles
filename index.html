At each vertex where tiles meet, the edges are "twisted" by the twist angle within the twist radius. Each edge gets a small tab at one end and a notch at the other — tiles softly interlock, easy to place from above but resistant to sliding apart. Tiles still meet at each vertex point (no voids). Tab depth ≈ r × sin(θ).
